# Getty Images — Metadata Rules & Best Practices

## TITLES

### Rules
- Maximum 60 characters (shorter titles generate better keyword suggestions in DeepMeta)
- Title Case throughout
- No trailing punctuation
- Describe exactly what is **visually present**
- Be specific — a precise title directly improves DeepMeta's controlled vocabulary suggestions
- Never include location terms already in Country/City metadata fields

### Best Practices
- Lead with the most specific subject (street name, building name, object type)
- Include neighbourhood where relevant
- Include material or type where it adds specificity (Enamel, Victorian, Ceramic)
- Avoid generic openers: "A view of...", "An image of..."

### Good vs Bad Examples
| Bad | Good |
|-----|------|
| Street Sign in London | Drayson Mews Enamel Street Sign, Kensington W.8 |
| Victorian Buildings | Holland Street Victorian Terrace Streetscape, Kensington |
| Ceramic Roundel | Della Robbia Ceramic Madonna Roundel, Drayson Mews |
| Mews Houses | Colourful Converted Mews Houses, Drayson Mews Kensington |
| Corner Building | Nos. 1–6 Hornton Street Victorian Mansion Block, Kensington |

## DESCRIPTIONS

### Format
Two sentences, minimum 120 words total.

**Sentence 1 — Editorial dateline + visual description (min. 60 words):**
```
Location, London — D Month YYYY: [precise visual description]
```
- En dash (—) not hyphen (-)
- Date: `7 March 2026` not `March 7` or `07/03/26`
- Present tense throughout
- No quotation marks in prose (use single quotes if needed)
- British English spelling (colour, centre, grey, organised)
- Describe: materials, typography, colours, architectural details, physical condition

**Sentence 2 — Specific context (min. 60 words):**
- Must be specific to THIS subject — never generic commentary
- Include: named architects, dates, borough history, street etymology, architectural movement, listed status
- Never: "Such signs are characteristic of upmarket London streets" (too generic)
- Always: specific facts a picture editor or researcher would find useful

### Full Example
> Kensington, London — 7 March 2026: A vitreous enamel street name sign for Drayson Mews, W.8, is fixed to a corner of weathered London stock brick, the white ground plate displaying the Royal Borough of Kensington in red blackletter script above DRAYSON MEWS in large black serif capitals, with the postcode district W.8 in red below; the green-edged metal backing shows verdigris patination at its corner fixings, and the glossy enamel surface remains fully intact. Drayson Mews lies off Pemberton Gardens in the former Royal Borough of Kensington — which merged with Chelsea in 1965 to form the present borough — and its street signs follow a typographic convention standardised in the early twentieth century, combining Gothic script for the borough header with roman capitals for the street name, a format now considered characteristic of west London's most prestigious residential districts.

### What NOT to do
- No internal quotation marks in prose
- No hyphen instead of en dash in dateline
- No past tense
- No American spellings
- No marketing language (stunning, beautiful, iconic)
- No uncertain attributions unless genuinely uncertain

## KEYWORDS

### Structure — 50 keywords per image

| Group | Keywords | Focus |
|-------|----------|-------|
| Primary (1–8) | Most specific and essential | Exact subject, material, key visual |
| Secondary (9–25) | Descriptive details | Materials, composition, viewpoint, setting |
| Tertiary (26–50) | Broader concepts | Culture, mood, architectural category |

### Getty Hierarchy Principle
Be **specific** to trigger broader terms automatically:
- "Victorian Terraced House" → Getty adds "House", "Building", "Architecture"
- "Cast Iron Street Lamp" → Getty adds "Lamp", "Street Furniture", "Metal"
- "Cobblestone" → Getty adds "Paving", "Street Surface"

### Always INCLUDE
- Specific subjects and variants
- Materials (Vitreous Enamel, London Stock Brick, Cast Iron, Wrought Iron, Cobblestone, Stucco, Glazed Terracotta)
- Composition: Close-up, Wide Shot, Detail Shot, Eye Level, Low Angle View, High Angle View, Perspective View
- Setting: Outdoors, Daytime, Overcast, Sunny, Winter, Spring, Summer, Autumn, Wet
- People: No People (for empty scenes)
- Concepts: British Culture, Heritage, Historic, Conservation Area, Urban, Traditional, English

### Always EXCLUDE
- Location names (London, England, United Kingdom, Europe) — auto-added from Country of Shoot
- File metadata (Horizontal, Vertical, Colour Image) — handled by other fields
- Editorial Use Only — handled separately
- Overly specific terms unlikely in Getty vocabulary (Granite Sett, Voussoir)
- Duplicates

## SIGN IDENTIFICATION

### Royal Borough of Kensington
- White vitreous enamel ground
- Red blackletter script: "The Royal Borough of Kensington"
- Black serif capitals for street name
- Red postcode district (W.8, SW3 etc.)
- Green-edged metal frame with corner screw fixings
- Verdigris patination common on older examples

### Vitreous Enamel (general)
- Smooth, glossy surface — glass fused to steel at high temperature
- Does not chip like paint — crazes in circular patterns if damaged
- Dark navy or green ground with white/cream lettering (non-Kensington)

### Ceramic Mosaic Tile
- Individual hand-set tiles visible on close inspection
- Slightly uneven surface, grout lines visible
- Common in former Metropolitan Borough of Hampstead (NW3)
- Dark ground, typically navy or black

### Painted Metal
- Flat matte surface — brush or spray marks may be visible
- **Never default to this without clear visual evidence**

## DEEPMETA SUBMISSION NOTES

- Country of Shoot = United Kingdom (set per batch)
- City = London (set per batch)
- Number of People = No People (architectural/sign shots)
- Date Created = read from EXIF automatically
- Keywords in READ-ONLY = accepted, system-generated
- Keywords in CANDIDATES = suggested, not yet confirmed
- Editorial images require place and date in description (dateline format above covers this)
